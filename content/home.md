## The Problem

The revenue cycle is a massive transactional labor engine — and revenue leaks invisibly because people never get to all of it.

### Denials
First-pass denial rates across healthcare RCM sit at roughly **5–15%** (published RCM industry research) — and the share that ever gets worked, much less appealed within timely-filing windows, is far lower.

### Aging A/R
Days in A/R typically run **35–55 days** (general healthcare RCM statistics). The aging report is always longer than the team that has to work it.

### Invisible Leakage
Underpayments at roughly **1–3% of net revenue** and recoverable coverage on roughly **5–10%** of self-pay accounts — leakage no one has capacity to chase.

## Platform Overview

A coordinated system of agents — not a model in a box.

"Agentic" here means concrete: agents that execute multi-step revenue-cycle workflows, orchestrated together, with evaluation gates, human-in-the-loop, and a complete audit trail.

- **Agentic execution** — Each agent ingests, reasons over rules and context, acts in connected systems, escalates to humans on defined conditions, and records every step.
- **Human-in-the-loop** — Outputs with regulatory exposure are reviewed by a person before they leave the system. Autonomy expands only as confidence accrues.
- **Evaluation gates** — An agent must clear evaluation thresholds before it goes live, and to stay live. Gating is the safety mechanism.
- **Audit & observability** — Every action and decision is logged and retained, with performance and drift monitored continuously.
- **Coordinated suite** — Agents share a common reference architecture so the kit behaves as one system and new agents come online on a steady cadence.
