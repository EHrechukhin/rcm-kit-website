## Hero

**Section:** INTEGRATIONS

### Headline
Works inside the systems you already run.

Agents execute the existing workflow in the existing stack — EHR/EMR, billing systems, clearinghouses, and payer channels. We meet your stack where it lives, hybrid-by-design where the payer hasn't caught up to EDI.

---

## 01 — EHR / EMR

**PointClickCare**  
SNF EHR/EMR — flagship integration depth for census, MDS, therapy, and ancillary data.

**Leading EHR/EMR platforms**  
Generic interfaces for hospital, health-system, and physician-group EHR/EMRs — connected as the engagement requires.

---

## 02 — Billing & Practice Management

**Practice-management systems**  
Read/write integration with the billing system of record for claims, posting, A/R, and patient billing.

**Charge-master & fee-schedule data**  
Reference data used by charge capture, posting, and underpayment detection.

---

## 03 — Clearinghouses & EDI

**Major clearinghouses**  
Submission and remittance pipelines for primary and secondary claims, status, and eligibility.

**EDI 837 / 837P / 837I** — Claim submission.

**EDI 835 / ERA** — Remittance and payment posting.

**EDI 276 / 277 / 277CA** — Claim status and acknowledgment.

**EDI 270 / 271** — Eligibility and benefit response.

---

## 04 — Payer Channels

**Direct payer EDI**  
Where the payer supports it — preferred for reliability and traceability.

**Payer-portal automation**  
Where EDI is not available — agents work the portals the team works today.

**IVR / call channels**  
For follow-up where status is only reachable by phone.

---

## 05 — Supporting Systems

**Bank deposit channels**  
Used by Payment Posting for daily bank reconciliation.

**Contract-terms repositories**  
Structured contract data feeds underpayment detection and posting reconciliation.

**Document & correspondence channels**  
Statement, appeal, and application generation and delivery.

---

## Integration Philosophy

Agents execute the existing workflow in the existing stack. Deployment is phased, agent by agent, with minimal disruption to the systems and people who depend on them. The integration surface is what changes — not the operating model.
